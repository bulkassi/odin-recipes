Classic apple Charlotte

Required ingredients (for 8 servings):

  • 3 firm-textured apples
  • 4 eggs
  • 200 g sugar
  • 100 g brown sugar
  • 120 g (4-5 tbsp) flour
  • 2 tbsp lemon juice

Optionally:

  • 0.5 tbsp powdered sugar

Cooking steps:

  1. Cut the apples into slices as shown in the photo (or thinner)
  2. Drizzle the sliced apples with lemon juice and mix well.
  3. Break eggs into a large bowl. Preheat the oven to 180 degrees Celsius.
  4. Add sugar to the eggs.
  5. Mix the egg-sugar mixture using a mixer on high speed for at least 8-10 minutes. The mass should become pale and increase several times in volume.
  6. Sift the flour into the mixture in portions.
  7. Gently fold in the flour fform bottom to top with lifting motions using a spatula to ensure the batter remains airy nd does not deflate.
  8. Line the bottom of the removable springform pan (23-24 cm in diameter) with parchment paper. There is no need to grease the sides of the pan.
  9. Place the apple slices on the bottom of the pan, then pour the batter over them.
  10. Bake the charlotte in a preheated oven set to 180 degrees Celsius for about 40 minutes.
  11. Allow the charlotte to cool completely in the pan, then turn the cake upside-down onto a plate so that the apple slices face upwards.
  12. When the dish has cooled completely, sprinkle it with powdered sugar.

Notes:

  • It's recommended not to pour all the sugar at once but gradually while beating.
  • In biscuit recipes, the amount of sugar in tablespoons often corresponds to the number of eggs. That means for a biscuit made with four eggs, 4-5 tablespoons (and an equal amount of flour) would suffice. Keep in mind that sugar affects not only the sweetness but also the structure and density of the batter.
  • The quality and speed of mixing may depend on the shape of the beaters, with paired main attachments being most effective.
